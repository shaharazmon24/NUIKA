# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Local customers in the Rehovot area, mostly families and food-lovers aged 25–50. They discover NUIKA through Instagram or word of mouth. They visit the site on mobile, once a week, to browse what's available and place a weekend order. The context is unhurried: a quiet Saturday morning scroll, coffee in hand. They want to feel like they are buying from a person, not a store.

## Product Purpose

A weekly boutique bread ordering site. Customers browse the current menu, add items to a running cart, and submit their order via WhatsApp. The site replaces a static flyer with a living, tactile experience that earns trust and makes ordering feel effortless.

## Brand Personality

Natural, organic, calm. The brand feels like it comes from a person with a real craft and a real kitchen — not a trend, not a business. Warmth is earned through texture, restraint, and honest copy, not decoration.

## Anti-references

- Supermarket chains (commercial, industrial, impersonal)
- Hipster coffee shops (too trendy, too black-and-white minimal, too self-conscious)
- Home baking blogs (amateur feel, text-heavy, no visual hierarchy)
- Fine dining restaurant sites (too formal, too dark, too expensive-feeling)

## Design Principles

1. **Person, not brand.** Every choice should feel like it came from a specific human with taste — not a design system or a category template.
2. **Restraint over decoration.** One beautiful thing per section beats five competing details. If an element doesn't earn its place, remove it.
3. **Touch before read.** The page should feel good before the user reads a word. Texture, spacing, and proportion carry the brand.
4. **Calm is the pace.** No urgency patterns, no countdown pressure, no flashing. The site should breathe.
5. **Keep the character.** The warm earth palette, the hand-drawn logo, the Hebrew serif — these are the identity. Upgrade the craft around them, not over them.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

- WCAG AA minimum (4.5:1 body text contrast)
- RTL Hebrew primary, LTR English toggle
- Respect prefers-reduced-motion on all animations
- Touch targets minimum 44x44px (mobile-first audience)
